Market Analysis • October 24, 2023 10:42 AM EST

Copper supply concerns deepen as Chilean labor talks stall amid rising global demand

Marcus Thorne

Senior Commodities Strategist • Reuters

The global copper market is bracing for a potential supply deficit as negotiations between unions and major mining operators in Chile's Atacama region reached an impasse late Tuesday.

Inventory Levels Critical

Inventories at London Metal Exchange (LME) registered warehouses have dropped to a 3-month low of 82,400 tonnes. This tightening of physical availability comes precisely as Chinese manufacturing data shows a stronger-than-expected rebound in industrial output for Q3.

Analysts at Goldman Sachs have revised their year-end price target for copper to $9,200 per tonne, up from $8,800, citing the "double whammy" of supply constraints and the green energy transition demand.

Impact on Related Markets

  • Chilean Peso (CLP): Strengthened 0.4% against the dollar as copper prices rose.
  • Mining Equities: Shares in Freeport-McMoRan and Antofagasta PLC saw pre-market gains of 2.1% and 1.8% respectively.
  • Tech Sector: Concerns over raw material costs for EV batteries may dampen margins for manufacturers in Q4.
